Career Mentorship

400 Points

   In December of last year and January of this year I completed an 18-hour mentorship with Doug Huber, the Vice President of Engineering at Indiana Automation, Inc. While I was with him, we visited many different factories and plants that his company had helped to automate. I was allowed to experiment with the software that he uses on a daily basis, as well as work in the shop and even do some simple wiring. I learned a great deal about my prospective field, Electrical Engineering. I was able to ask Doug and several of his associates about fields of specialization, and I gave myself a clearer picture of what I need to study in college. I also learned that Engineering is the right career for me, which I can only do through personal experience. I liked the hands-on aspect of that line of work. I thoroughly enjoyed my mentorship experience, and I highly recommend it to any other interested students. Other than having fun and meeting new people, it is an excellent way to learn if a certain career is right for you. It also gives you a contact in your field of expertise, someone you can count on later if you have any questions.
