Recommended Reading


 

Fiction


Harry, Eric L. Society of the Mind. New York, HarperCollins, 1996.

An intriguing book with a futuristic scenario of what the computer can do. Clearly describes artificial intelligence and why computers pose a threat to biological evolution.


Nonfiction


Hancock, LynNell. ÒThe Haves and the Have-Nots.Ó Newsweek 27 February 1995: 50-53.

A complete description of the problems of unequal computer access. Provides multiple case studies, statistics & charts, and possible solutions.


Iovine, John. Robots, Androids, and Animatrons. New York: McGraw-Hill, 1998.

Contains descriptions of robotics, artificial intelligence, and possible uses of each. Provides experiments and instructions for building several types of robots.


Negroponte, Nicholas. Being Digital. New York: Alfred A. Knopf, 1995.

A clearly written book describing the future and what it holds in store. Contains definitions and uses for new technologies, also tells how technology will someday change our culture.


Nonprint


Jay Kay. Virtual Insanity. Perf. Jamiroquai. Compact disc. EMI Music, 1996.

A lively song describing the problems that technology causes upon our society.
