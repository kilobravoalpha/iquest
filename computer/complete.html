Complete List of Works Cited


 

Fiction

Bronson, Po. The First $20 Million Is Always the Hardest: A Silicone Valley Novel. New York: Random House, 1997.

Clarke, Arthur C. 2001: A Space Odyssey. New York: Penguin Books, 1968.

Harry, Eric L. Society of the Mind. New York: HarperCollins, 1996. An intriguing book with a futuristic scenario of what computers can do. Clearly describes artificial intelligence and why computers pose a threat to biological evolution.

Franklet, Duane. Bad Memory. New York: Simon & Schuster Inc., 1997.

Nonfiction

Bailey, James. After Thought: The Computer Challenge to Human Intelligence. New York: HarperCollins, 1996.

Barnhart, Clarence L. Ed. World Book Dictionary, The. Chicago: World Book Inc., 1990.

Canter, Sheryl. "The Connected School." PC Magazine 10 February 1998: 23.

Cetron, Marvin J. Probable Tomorrows: How Science and Technology Will Transform Our Liven in the Next Twenty Years. New York: St. Martin's Press, 1997.

Chapman, Gary. "Nurturing Neighborhood Nets." Technology Review October 1997: 48-54.

"Digital." PC Webopaedia. 1 September 1996. Internet. Available: http://www.sandybay.com/pc-web/digital.htm. 14 December 1997.

Farah, Mounir A. World History: The Human Experience. New York: Glencoe/McGraw-Hill, 1997.

Gooden, Andrea R. Computers in the Classroom. Cupertino, California: Apple Press, 1996.

Hancock, LynNell. "The Haves and the Have-Nots." Newsweek 27 February 1995: 50-53. A complete description of the problems of unequal computer access. Provides multiple case studies, statistics & charts, and possible solutions.

Ihnatko, Andy. Cyberspeak. New York: Random House, 1997.

Iovine, John. Robots, Androids, and Animatrons. New York: McGraw-Hill, 1998. Contains descriptions of robotics, artificial intelligence, and possible uses of each. Provides experiments and instructions for building several types of robots.

Marriott, Michel. "Putting Your Best Fear Forward." Newsweek 27 February 1995: 53.

McGowan, Alan H. "A Pause in the Revolution." Environment 1 December 1996: 1.

"Nearly Half of US Homes Have Computers." @ Internet Magazine April 1998: 14.

Negroponte, Nicholas. Being Digital. New York: Alfred A. Knopf, 1995. A clearly written book describing the future and what it holds in store. Contains definitions and uses for new technologies, also tells how technology will someday change our culture.

New Book of Popular Science, The. Danbury, Connecticut: Grolier, 1996.

Postman, Neil. Technopoly: The Surrender of Culture to Technology. New York: Alfred A. Knopf, 1993.

Ratan, Suneel. "A New Divide Between Haves and Have Nots?" Time Spring 1995: 25-26.

Richman, Ellen. Spotlight On Computer Literacy. New York: Random House, 1985.

"Roboflops." The Economist 19 October 1996: 86.

Samuelson, Robert J. "The Myth of Cyber Inequality." Newsweek 27 February 1995: 55.

Steen, Douglas R. Teaching With the Internet. Bellevue, Washington: Resolution Business Press, 1995.

Tenner, Edward. Why Things Bite Back: Technology and the Revenge of Unintended Consequences. New York: Knopf, 1996.

Weaver, Pam. "Training Courses: A roundup of Internet and Computer Training Classes." @ Internet Magazine April 1998: 7.

Woodstock, JoAnne, Ed. Microsoft Press Computer Dictionary. Redmond, Washington: Microsoft Press, 1991.

Zygmont, Jeffrey. "Inside an Auto Plant." Omni November 1992: 22.

Nonprint

Jay Kay. Virtual Insanity. Perf. Jamiroquai. Compact disc. EMI Music, 1996. A lively song describing the problems that technology causes upon our society.
